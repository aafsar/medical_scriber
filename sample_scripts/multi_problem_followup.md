# Multi-Problem Follow-up — Test Script

> **Scenario:** 65-year-old male follow-up for diabetes management and hypertension, with a new complaint of heartburn/GERD symptoms. Family history intentionally NOT discussed to test sentinel values.
> **Expected duration:** ~4-5 minutes
> **Key terms to verify:** hemoglobin A1c, metformin, glipizide, lisinopril, hydrochlorothiazide, omeprazole, gastroesophageal reflux, creatinine, BUN, systolic, diastolic, SpO2, neuropathy, retinopathy, microalbuminuria

---

**Doctor:** "Good morning, Mr. Davis. Good to see you again. It's been about three months since your last visit. How have things been going?"

**Patient:** "Hi, Dr. Kim. Things have been okay, but I do have a couple of things to talk about today."

**Doctor:** "Sure, let's go through everything. What's on your mind?"

**Patient:** "Well, first, I wanted to check on my diabetes numbers. I've been checking my sugars at home and they've been running a little high, like one forty to one sixty fasting. Also, I've been getting this burning feeling in my chest and throat, especially after dinner and when I lie down at night. It's been going on about three weeks."

**Doctor:** "Let's address each of those. Let me start with the diabetes. You said your fasting sugars have been one forty to one sixty — how about after meals?"

**Patient:** "After meals they go up to about two hundred, sometimes two twenty."

**Doctor:** "Are you taking your metformin regularly?"

**Patient:** "Yes, metformin one thousand milligrams twice a day, I haven't missed any doses."

**Doctor:** "Good. Any episodes of low blood sugar — shakiness, sweating, feeling like you might pass out?"

**Patient:** "No, nothing like that."

**Doctor:** "Any numbness or tingling in your feet?"

**Patient:** "A little bit of tingling in my toes, actually. I've noticed it in the last month or so."

**Doctor:** "That's important — that could be early diabetic neuropathy. We'll check on that. When was your last eye exam?"

**Patient:** "About a year ago. They said everything was fine, no retinopathy."

**Doctor:** "Good. Now let's talk about the heartburn. You said it's a burning feeling in your chest and throat. Does it come up after eating?"

**Patient:** "Yes, especially after dinner, and it's worse when I lie down. Sometimes I get a sour taste in the back of my throat."

**Doctor:** "Any difficulty swallowing, vomiting, or blood in your stool?"

**Patient:** "No, none of those."

**Doctor:** "Any specific foods that trigger it?"

**Patient:** "Spicy food and coffee seem to make it worse. And tomato sauce."

**Doctor:** "How often is this happening?"

**Patient:** "Almost every night for the past three weeks."

**Doctor:** "Okay. And how about your blood pressure? Any headaches, dizziness, or vision changes?"

**Patient:** "No headaches or vision changes. I feel fine otherwise."

**Doctor:** "What are all the medications you're currently on?"

**Patient:** "Metformin one thousand milligrams twice daily, lisinopril twenty milligrams once daily, and hydrochlorothiazide twelve point five milligrams once daily."

**Doctor:** "Any allergies?"

**Patient:** "Sulfa drugs — I get hives."

**Doctor:** "And do you smoke or drink?"

**Patient:** "I quit smoking twenty years ago. I don't drink."

**Doctor:** "Alright, let me do your exam. Your vitals today: blood pressure is one forty-eight over ninety-two, heart rate seventy-eight, respiratory rate fourteen, temperature ninety-eight point two, oxygen saturation — SpO2 — ninety-seven percent, and your weight is two hundred and ten pounds, BMI thirty-one point four."

**Doctor:** "You look well overall. Heart sounds regular, no murmurs. Lungs are clear. Abdomen is soft, mildly tender in the epigastric area when I press here — does that hurt?"

**Patient:** "A little, yes."

**Doctor:** "No guarding, no rebound. Bowel sounds are active. Let me check your feet — I'm going to test sensation with this monofilament. Can you feel this?"

**Patient:** "Barely on the toes, but I can feel it on the rest of my foot."

**Doctor:** "That confirms some decreased sensation in the toes bilaterally. Pulses in your feet are intact."

**Doctor:** "I have your lab results from last week. Your hemoglobin A1c came back at eight point two percent — that's up from seven point one three months ago. Your fasting glucose was one fifty-three. Creatinine is one point one, BUN is eighteen — kidney function looks good. We also checked a urine microalbumin, and that was mildly elevated at forty-two micrograms per milligram. Lipid panel looks okay — LDL is ninety-five."

**Doctor:** "Alright, let me summarize where we are. I see three things to address today."

**Doctor:** "Number one, your type two diabetes. Your hemoglobin A1c has gone from seven point one to eight point two, which tells me the metformin alone isn't enough anymore. Your home glucose readings confirm that. You're also showing early signs of diabetic neuropathy with the tingling and decreased monofilament sensation, and the mildly elevated microalbumin suggests early diabetic kidney changes. I'd like to add glipizide, five milligrams once daily with breakfast. We'll recheck your A1c in three months. I also want you to see the eye doctor again for a diabetic retinopathy screening, and I'll refer you to a podiatrist for your feet."

**Doctor:** "Number two, your hypertension. Your blood pressure today is one forty-eight over ninety-two, which is above goal. The systolic and diastolic are both elevated. I'd like to increase your lisinopril from twenty to forty milligrams daily. Keep the hydrochlorothiazide the same for now. I want you to check your blood pressure at home twice a week and bring a log to your next visit."

**Doctor:** "Number three, the heartburn. Your symptoms — the burning after meals, worse lying down, sour taste in the throat — are very consistent with gastroesophageal reflux disease, or GERD. I'd like to start you on omeprazole twenty milligrams once daily, take it thirty minutes before breakfast. I also want you to avoid eating within three hours of bedtime, elevate the head of your bed, and cut back on the coffee, spicy food, and tomato sauce. If symptoms don't improve in four weeks, we may need to do further evaluation."

**Doctor:** "Continue your other medications. Watch for any signs of low blood sugar with the new glipizide — if you feel shaky or sweaty, eat something and check your sugar. Any questions?"

**Patient:** "Should I be worried about the kidney thing you mentioned?"

**Doctor:** "The microalbumin elevation is very mild and we caught it early. That's one reason I'm increasing the lisinopril — ACE inhibitors like lisinopril help protect the kidneys in diabetic patients. We'll recheck the microalbumin in three months. If we keep your blood sugar and blood pressure well controlled, we can prevent it from progressing."

**Patient:** "Okay, that's reassuring. Thank you, doctor."

**Doctor:** "You're welcome. Let's plan to see you back in three months. If the heartburn doesn't improve in four weeks, call the office and we can move up your appointment. Take care."
